Refining Box Annotations into High-Quality Pseudo Masks for Fire and Smoke Detection

Cao Thanh Bang and Luong Duc Vinh1

1VTS – vinhld2@viettel.com.vn – 0355400785

Report Code Demo

TL;DR • Bounding boxes are a poor fit for fire and smoke, which are irregular, semi-transparent, and constantly changing shape. We propose a SAM2-assisted pseudo-labeling framework that turns the box-only annotations of FASDD_CV (95,314 images) into pixel-accurate masks • no manual pixel labeling involved. An escalating cascade of four refinement generators recovers boxes that SAM2 misses on the first pass, and the resulting masks are used as auxiliary supervision for YOLO11m-Seg, improving localization quality (mAP50-95) by +5.1 points over box-only training.

Motivation

Boxes are the wrong shape for fire and smoke

Most large-scale fire and smoke benchmarks, including FASDD_CV, are annotated only with bounding boxes, since pixel-level labeling at this scale is too expensive. But flames and smoke are variant, transparent, and often occupy only a small fraction of their enclosing box • so every box carries substantial background noise as supervision, which limits how tightly a detector can learn to localize the true object extent.

Contributions

Core Contributions

01

SAM2-Assisted Pseudo-Labeling

Every existing box is treated as a prompt for SAM2, and mask generation is framed as a candidate-selection problem • scored by how much of the box area a candidate mask recovers • eliminating the need for any additional pixel-level manual labeling.

02

Progressive Cascaded Refinement

Boxes SAM2 fails to cover on the first pass are escalated through four increasingly specialized generators • midpoint prompting, crop-and-resize, an HSV heuristic for fire, and GrabCut for smoke • recovering reliable masks through iterative prompting rather than accepting the first result.

03

Consistent Detection & Segmentation Gains

The refined masks provide richer supervision for both box-based and mask-based training: YOLO11m-Seg with pseudo-mask supervision reaches the strongest boundary-aware localization (mAP50-95) of any model tested, while also enabling a segmentation-only training paradigm.

Method

Cascaded Pseudo-Mask Generation

Existing ground-truth boxes are converted into SAM2 masks; boxes with insufficient coverage are recovered through an escalating cascade of refinement strategies before training YOLO11m.

Stage 1 • Box-Prompted Mask Generation

Every box is put into SAM2 as a bounding-box prompt, producing an initial candidate mask. Since no ground-truth mask exists to check against, each candidate is matched back to its source box by centroid distance, and a box is considered covered only once a matched mask satisfies both the distance threshold and a minimum box-coverage score.

Stage 2 • Cascaded Refinement

Boxes that remain unmatched are escalated through four generators, tried in order, until coverage is sufficient. Only the best-scoring candidate across all four is kept, and it is accepted as a pseudo-label only once it covers at least 15% of the source box.

GeneratorStrategyApplies toTriggered when
G1 Box + midpoint SAM2 prompt Fire & smoke Always tried first
G2 Crop (30% padding), resize to 512², re-prompt Fire & smoke Box area < 1.5% of image and G1 coverage < 0.40
G3 HSV color threshold + morphological cleanup Fire only Coverage still < 0.25 after G1 / G2

Across the 57,961 boxes recovered during Stage 2, G1 alone accounts for 96.1% of cases • most boxes missed in Stage 1 stem from an ambiguous initial prompt. G2, G3, and G4 contribute smaller but complementary gains on small, fire-colored, and diffuse-smoke instances respectively; only 0.1% of boxes remain unrecovered after the full cascade. The refinement process lifts full per-image coverage from 42.1% to 99.8%, and the overall pseudo-label failure rate after refinement is 3.64%.
