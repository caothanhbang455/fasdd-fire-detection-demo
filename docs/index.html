TL;DR — We address fire and smoke detection from surveillance video on the large-scale FASDD_CV dataset (95,314 images). Rather than relying on expensive pixel-level annotations, we generate auxiliary segmentation masks via a 4-tier SAM2 cascade (box prompt → crop+resize → HSV → GrabCut), then train YOLO11m-seg with joint detection and segmentation supervision, reaching mAP50 = 86.97%. For video deployment, we integrate a FLAME-inspired post-processing pipeline that suppresses false positives through background subtraction and fire-specific motion trajectory analysis — with no additional retraining.

Motivation

Why is this hard?

Fire and smoke detection from surveillance cameras must contend with high false positive rates from visually similar phenomena — sunsets, headlights, industrial steam — and scarce pixel-level annotations at scale.

🔴 The FP Problem

Our detection baseline produces 902 false positives from 6,533 "Neither" images — scenes with sunset gradients, vehicle headlights, and smoke-like clouds that confuse per-frame classifiers without temporal context.

🔴 No Pixel Annotations

FASDD_CV provides only bounding-box labels. Training a segmentation head for sharper localization requires either costly manual masks or a robust pseudo-labeling strategy at scale.

🟢 Our Solution: Auxiliary Segmentation

SAM2 generates pseudo-masks via a 4-tier cascade, validated by spatial matching rather than index order. YOLO11m-seg learns jointly — tighter mask boundaries improve feature quality for both tasks.

🟢 Our Solution: Temporal Post-Processing

Following FLAME (Gragnaniello et al., 2025), we add background subtraction + motion trajectory analysis as a post-processing stage after YOLO inference — no retraining, deployable on fixed surveillance cameras.

Contributions

Core Contributions

🪄

4-Tier SAM2 Mask Cascade

A resumable, checkpointed cascade that iterates through four increasingly specialized strategies to generate pseudo-masks for boxes that SAM2 fails on the first attempt — with spatial centroid matching to avoid index-order bugs.

🔥

Joint Detection + Segmentation

YOLO11m-seg trained with auxiliary segmentation supervision from SAM2 pseudo-masks. Mask quality is audited for duplicates, degenerate polygons, and coverage gaps before training.

🎥

FLAME Temporal Post-Processing

Background subtraction gates out static FP regions; fire-specific motion trajectory analysis discards detections whose dynamics are inconsistent with real fire or smoke — both as a plug-in wrapper around any YOLO checkpoint.

Method

Three-Phase Pipeline

1
Colab · SAM2

SAM2 Pseudo-Mask Generation

Starting from FASDD_CV's bounding-box annotations only, we prompt SAM2 to produce instance segmentation masks. A critical finding: SAM2 does not guarantee that output mask order matches input box order when given multiple boxes simultaneously. We fix this with a spatial centroid-matching step (match_segs_to_gt) before writing any polygon to disk. Images where SAM2 fails to produce a mask above coverage ≥ 0.15 are sent through a 4-tier fallback cascade.

2
Kaggle · YOLO11m-seg

Joint Detection + Segmentation Training

Pseudo-masks are cleaned (dedup, degenerate removal, coordinate clamping) and zipped as a Kaggle dataset. YOLO11m-seg is trained with joint detection and segmentation loss. The auxiliary segmentation signal from SAM2 masks encourages the backbone to learn tighter, boundary-aware features — improving detection even when masks are imperfect.

3
Deployment · FLAME

Temporal FP Suppression (FLAME)

For video input, YOLO inference alone produces false alarms on sunset glows, vehicle headlights, and smoke-like clouds that appear for only 1–2 frames. Following FLAME (Gragnaniello et al., Neural Computing and Applications, 2025), we add a two-stage model-based motion analysis after YOLO as a pure post-processing wrapper — no retraining required.

Phase 1 Deep Dive

4-Tier SAM2 Mask Cascade

For each GT box that has no matching polygon above the quality threshold, tiers are tried in order. The cascade stops at the first tier reaching coverage ≥ 0.15, or keeps the best across all four.

T2 Crop & Resize to 512px Fire & Smoke bbox area < 1.5% of image Tiny fire objects get lost in full-resolution SAM2 context
T3 HSV color threshold Fire only T1 & T2 fail Fire occupies a compact HSV range (H∈[0°,35°], high S & V)

Phase 3 Deep Dive · FLAME

Temporal FP Suppression

Following FLAME: fire detection in videos combining a deep neural network with a model-based motion analysis (Gragnaniello et al., Neural Computing and Applications, 2025), we implement a two-stage post-processing wrapper around YOLO inference for video input. Both stages are model-based and require no retraining of the detector.

Stage 1 — Background Subtraction

A Gaussian Mixture Model (GMM / MOG2) builds a per-pixel background model from the first ~100 frames. Any YOLO detection whose foreground mask has insufficient motion relative to the background model is immediately discarded — eliminating persistent fire-colored objects that are part of the scene itself (factory lighting, permanently lit signs, sunset gradients on a fixed wall).

Why this works for fixed cameras: Surveillance cameras at Viettel installations are static. The background model converges in <5 seconds of footage and remains stable. A sunset on the horizon appears in the same pixel coordinates every evening — the background model learns it and it is immediately gated out.

Stage 2 — Motion Trajectory Analysis

Detections that pass Stage 1 are tracked across frames (ByteTrack). The trajectory of each tracked detection is validated against a tunable model of fire-specific dynamics: fire persists for multiple frames, tends to grow in area, and moves upward (smoke rises); false alarms from vehicle headlights appear for 1–2 frames and disappear, or remain spatially fixed without the characteristic flickering motion signature.

Tunable per scenario: FLAME's motion model exposes thresholds for minimum track lifetime, area growth rate, and spatial displacement — allowing calibration to the specific installation environment without retraining the detector.

How FLAME addresses our 902 FP cases

Sunset / horizon glow

Static position, no foreground motion → eliminated by Stage 1

Vehicle headlights

1–2 frame flash, does not persist → eliminated by Stage 2 (min track lifetime)

Industrial steam / cloud

Motion does not match upward fire trajectory → eliminated by Stage 2 (trajectory model)

Real fire / smoke

Persistent, growing, fire-like dynamics → passes both stages → Alert
